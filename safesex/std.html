Sexually Transmitted Infections

Sexually transmitted infections (or sexually transmitted diseases) are infections that can be contracted through sexual contact. These infections can be prevented through the use of some contraceptives. Some STIs can be treated or may go away after some time, while others are permanent.

Genital Herpes

  • Where is it found? Genitals and/or Rectum.
  • How is it spread? Oral, anal, and vaginal sex, as well as contact with infected skin.
  • What are the symptoms? Often no symptoms are present, but when present there are painful blisters or sores, fever and swollen glands may occur. Symptoms can come back later.
  • What is the treatment? No cure. However, there is treatment to reduce symptoms.

Hepatitis B

  • Where is it found? Blood, semen and vaginal fluids.
  • How is it spread? Oral, anal, and vaginal sex. It can also be passed from mother to child.
  • What are the symptoms? Often there are no obvious symptoms, however jaundice, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, fatigue, joint pain are all symptoms. Hep-B can also lead to liver cancer and liver failure.
  • What is the treatment? No cure. However, can treat infections caused by disease.

Gonorrhea

  • Where is it found? Vagina, Cervix, Urethra, throat, and rectum.
  • How is it spread? Oral, anal, and vaginal sex. It can also be passed from mother to child.
  • What are the symptoms?
    • Males: often have no symptoms, may be burning or pain with urination.
    • Females: often have no symptoms, may have vaginal discharge, may lead to infertility.
  • What is the treatment? Antibiotics.

Chlamydia

  • Where is it found? Vagina, throat, discharge from Penis, and rectum.
  • How is it spread? Oral, anal, and vaginal sex. It can also be passed from mother to child.
  • What are the symptoms? burning or pain when peeing, discharge from penis and vagina, and infertilitiy
  • What is the treatment? Antibiotics.

Syphilis

  • Where is it found? Genital area, mouth, skin, anus and rectum.
  • How is it spread? Oral, anal, and vaginal sex, and contact with sores. It can also be passed from mother to child.
  • What are the symptoms? Painless sores, which when untreated may spread to brain and/or heart. May cause stillbirths.
  • What is the treatment? Antibiotics.

HIV/AIDS

  • Where is it found? Blood, Semen, Vaginal Fluid, and Breast Milk.
  • How is it spread? Oral, anal, and vaginal sex. It can also be passed from mother to child.
  • What are the symptoms? Causes flu-like symptoms which is the result of a weakened immune system. Casues severe infections as well.
  • What is the treatment? No cure. Lifetime treatment required to control symptoms.
