Contraception

Contraceptives are tools that you can use to practive safe sex. They aid against unplanned pregnancies and contracting sexually transmitted diseases. Below is a list of many of the common forms of contraception.

Withdrawal (Pull Out Method)

A man who uses withdrawal will pull his penis out of the vagina before ejaculation.

  • Withdrawal is somewhere between 73% - 96% effective, depending on the self control of each partner.

Condom

Condoms are thin, stretchy pouches that you wear on your penis during sex.

    What is it?
  • Condoms are the only type of birth control out there that also help protect against STDs.
  • If you use condoms perfectly every single time you have sex, they're 98% effective at preventing pregnancy. But there's a small chance that you will get pregnant even if you always use them the right way.
  • Roll the condom on when your penis is hard, but BEFORE it touches your partner — and wear it the whole time you're having sex.

Birth Control Pills

Birth control pills are a kind of medication that women can take daily to prevent pregnancy.

    How does it work?
  • Keeping eggs from leaving the ovaries. Pregnancy cannot happen if there is no egg to join with sperm.
  • Making cervical lining thicker. This keeps sperm from getting to the eggs.
  • Less than 1 out of 100 women will get pregnant each year if they always take the pill each day as directed.

Spermicide

Spermicide contains chemicals that stop your sperm from moving.

    How does it work?
  • Spermicides are inserted deep into the vagina shortly before sex in the form of creams, foams, and gels.
  • 15% of women will become pregnant each year if they use spermicide.
  • Some condoms also contain a spermicide lubricant for extra effectiveness.

Birth Control Vaginal Ring

The ring is a small, flexible ring a woman inserts into her vagina once a month to prevent pregnancy. It is left in place for three weeks and taken out for the remaining week each month.

  • The hormones in the ring are the same hormones as in the birth control pill — estrogen and progestin.
  • Less than 1 out of 100 women will get pregnant each year if they always use the ring as directed.
  • The ring can be used to control when and how often you have your period. It can also be used continuously to eliminate monthly periods.

Diaphragm

The diaphragm is a shallow, dome-shaped, silicon cup with a flexible rim. You insert it into the vagina and it covers the cervix.

    How does it work?
  • Diaphragms keep sperm from joining with an egg. In order to be as effective as possible, the diaphragm must be used with spermicide.
  • If women always use the diaphragm as directed, 6 out of 100 will become pregnant each year.

Vasectomy

A vasectomy is considered a permanent form of birth control for men.

    How does it work?
  • During vasectomy, the tubes that carry sperm are blocked or closed. When the tubes are closed, sperm cannot leave a man's body.
  • A vasectomy is the most effective birth control for men. It is nearly 100 percent effective.
  • Most men can have a vasectomy safely.

Morning-After Pill (Emergency Contraception)

The Morning-After Pill, or Plan-B, is a safe and effective way to prevent pregnancy after unprotected intercourse.

    When should I take it?
  • If there was no use of contraception when you had sex, your condom broke, your partner didn't pull out in time, or you were forced to have sex, you may consider taking Plan-B if you do not wish to become pregnant.
