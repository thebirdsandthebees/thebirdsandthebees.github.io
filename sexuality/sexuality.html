Sexuality

Don't worry, it's completely normal to be curious about sex. In fact, this curiosity is a natural and healthy part of life that everyone goes through. You're not alone. And you're definitely not the only one with questions.

Sexual Orientation:

Who are you attracted to? Boys, girls, both?

  • If you are attracted to the opposite gender, you are heterosexual
  • If you are attracted to the same gender, you are homosexual
  • If you are attracted to both genders, you are bisexual
  • There are other terms for other types of attraction, such as asexuality, pansexuality, polysexuality and so on. But the 3 mentioned above are the most common.

Sexual Expression:

What do you think/say/do?
Here are examples of things that might reflect your sexuality:

  • The way you dress, talk, or move through the world
  • Your relationships with others, and the way and amount you connect physically or emotionally with other people
  • The kinds of things you read, the movies and TV you watch, the music you listen to
  • Sexual activities alone or with a partner or partners
  • Where you shop and what you buy
  • Who you vote for, and organizations you support

Sexual Health

How can we enjoy our sexuality?

  • Understanding that sexuality is a natural part of life and is more than sexual behavior
  • Knowing and respecting everyone's sexual rights
  • Having access to sexual health information, education, and care
  • Being safe and making the right choices—before, during and after sex
  • Being able to experience sexual pleasure, satisfaction, and intimacy when needed
  • Being able to openly discuss our sexual health with others, such as partners or doctors
