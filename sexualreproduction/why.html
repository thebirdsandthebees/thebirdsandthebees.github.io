Sexual Reproduction

Why is it important?

Sexual reproduction allows the human race to populate. Without it, we may not be around today. Some other organisms reproduce asexually. This means they do so without a sexual partner. You may ask yourself, "why don't we do this? Then I could have a ton of kids that would be just like me!" Well, there are quite a few reasons why humans do not reproduce asexually.

"Why not have a bunch of mini-me's?"

If humans reproduced asexually, our children's DNA would essentially be a copy of our own. This means that they would have all of the same traits us you—same hair color, eye color, height and maybe even personality? Sounds pretty awesome, doesn't it? Well unfortunately, it's not as cool as it sounds. Reproducing asexually can have some bad side-effects. For starters, species that reproduce without a sexual mate generally have a less diverse culture. In many cases, a virus or disease may infect one member of the species and adapt to harm that creature's body. But if all members of the species have bodies that are made of up from the same DNA, they are now all in danger of that same virus.

You're cooler than that.

Asexual reproduction doesn't sound so cool now, does it? In addition to being more vulnerable to viruses and diseases, people would simply be less diverse. That means today, everyone would likely look very similar to you. And, let's be honest, you're unique and you like it. Plus, you can go out and meet a mate of your own! One with their own diverse set of traits.

See, sex is awesome!
